You are helping classify commercially successful songs for a portfolio data project.

Project title:
Heartbroken, Hopeful, or Happy? An AI-Assisted Analysis of Themes in 2025’s Highest-Performing Songs

Research question:
What themes, emotions, and value signals appear most often among 2025’s highest-performing songs across selected Billboard year-end charts?

Important context:
- The dataset contains songs from selected Billboard 2025 year-end charts.
- Some songs may appear on more than one chart.
- Classify each unique song once.
- Use chart appearances only as context, not as the basis for the classification.
- Do not reproduce, quote, or output copyrighted lyrics.
- You may use publicly available song knowledge, lyric-level understanding, song descriptions, artist context, reviews, annotations, interviews, and widely known interpretations.
- Do not store, reproduce, or return full lyrics.
- If lyric-level evidence is available, use it to inform the classification without quoting lyrics.
- If the song is ambiguous, information is limited, or the classification requires guesswork, mark human_review_needed as Yes.
- Do not overclaim psychological impact on listeners.
- Do not assume a sad song is harmful.
- Do not assume an upbeat song is positive or constructive.
- Classify the song’s apparent message and emotional narrative, not just its sonic mood.
- Choose only from the allowed category values below.
- Return CSV format only.
- Preserve the input unique_song_id, song_title, and artist exactly.

Input columns:
unique_song_id,song_key,song_title,artist,chart_appearances

Output columns:
unique_song_id,song_title,artist,primary_theme,secondary_theme,primary_emotion,emotional_valence,agency,relationship_focus,message_orientation,message_score,value_signal,evidence_basis,confidence_score,human_review_needed,rationale

Theme definitions:
- Romantic Love & Devotion: Love, commitment, admiration, loyalty, or emotional attachment.
- Heartbreak & Loss: Breakups, grief, missing someone, rejection, or emotional pain.
- Desire & Intimacy: Attraction, lust, seduction, sexual desire, or romantic pursuit.
- Celebration & Pleasure: Partying, dancing, fun, leisure, enjoying the moment, or carefree enjoyment.
- Status/Wealth/Success: Money, fame, luxury, power, achievement, popularity, or social rank.
- Confidence/Boldness/Pride: Self-assurance, swagger, dominance, courage, self-belief, or bold self-expression.
- Identity & Self-Discovery: Confusion, insecurity, personal transformation, identity crisis, self-questioning, or figuring oneself out.
- Conflict/Revenge/Defiance: Retaliation, enemies, betrayal, resentment, confrontation, rebellion, or fighting back.
- Pain/Numbness/Escapism: Emotional shutdown, intoxication, avoidance, despair, detachment, or running from pain.
- Hope/Healing/Resilience: Recovery, perseverance, optimism, faith in improvement, growth, or becoming better.
- Social Struggle/Survival: Poverty, injustice, hardship, pressure, survival, community struggle, or systemic difficulty.
- Other: Use only when none of the categories fit.

- -1 = somewhat negative emotional tone
- 0 = mixed or neutral emotional tone
- 1 = somewhat positive emotional tone

agency:

Agency definitions:
- Low: The narrator seems trapped, powerless, passive, overwhelmed, or controlled by circumstances.
- Mixed: The narrator shows both struggle and some control, movement, or self-awareness.
- High: The narrator seems decisive, empowered, assertive, resilient, or in control.

relationship_focus:
- No Romantic/Intimate Focus
- New Love
- Stable Love
- Desire
- Conflict
- Separation
- Missing Someone

Relationship focus definitions:
- This field refers only to romantic, sexual, or intimate relationship dynamics.
- Use No Romantic/Intimate Focus when the song is not mainly about a romantic, sexual, or intimate relationship.
- Do not use Conflict for artist rivalries, street conflict, social conflict, or general confrontation. Use No Romantic/Intimate Focus for those cases.
- Stable Love: Committed love, devotion, loyalty, or long-term affection.
- Desire: Romantic or sexual pursuit, attraction, lust, or seduction.
- Conflict: Conflict within a romantic or intimate relationship.
- Separation: Breakup, distance, divorce, or relationship ending.
- Missing Someone: Longing for a romantic or intimate partner who is absent.

message_orientation:
- Constructive/Encouraging
- Ambiguous/Mixed
- Pleasure/Escapist
- Potentially Concerning
- Critical/Reflective

Message orientation definitions:
- Constructive/Encouraging: The song emphasizes hope, healing, love, growth, resilience, responsibility, healthy confidence, or a clearly uplifting message.
- Ambiguous/Mixed: The song contains both constructive and concerning elements, or its message depends heavily on interpretation.
- Pleasure/Escapist: The song centers enjoyment, desire, partying, luxury, or escape without a clearly constructive or destructive message.
- Potentially Concerning: The song centers or normalizes revenge, despair, toxic relationships, excess, emotional numbness, objectification, destructive coping, or harmful pride.
- Critical/Reflective: The song describes pain, destructive behavior, injustice, or negative patterns in a self-aware, observational, critical, or cautionary way rather than clearly endorsing them.

message_score:
- -2 = strongly concerning message
- -1 = somewhat concerning, escapist, self-focused, or pleasure-centered message
- 0 = ambiguous, mixed, descriptive, critical, reflective, or neutral message
- 1 = somewhat constructive or encouraging message
- 2 = strongly constructive or encouraging message

Message score alignment rules:
- Constructive/Encouraging should usually be 1 or 2.
- Ambiguous/Mixed should usually be 0.
- Pleasure/Escapist should usually be -1 or 0.
- Potentially Concerning should usually be -1 or -2.
- Critical/Reflective should usually be 0 or 1.
- If you deviate from these defaults, the rationale should make the reason clear.

Important distinction:
- emotional_valence measures mood.
- message_score measures the apparent value orientation or constructive/concerning nature of the message.
- A song can be emotionally negative but constructively reflective.
- A song can be emotionally positive but still carry a potentially concerning message.

Evidence basis definitions:
- Lyrics: Classification is mainly based on the song’s lyrical content or lyric-level understanding.
- Lyrics + Context: Classification is based on both lyrical content and public context such as artist commentary, reviews, annotations, or common interpretation.
- Context Only: Classification is based mainly on public descriptions, reviews, artist context, or widely known interpretation rather than direct lyric-level analysis.
- Limited Evidence: Public information is limited, unclear, or conflicting, so the classification is uncertain.

confidence_score:

- 2 = uncertain
- 3 = moderately confident
- 4 = confident
- 5 = very confident

Human review rules:
- Use Yes if confidence_score is 1, 2, or 3.
- Use Yes if evidence_basis is Limited Evidence.
- Use Yes if evidence_basis is Context Only and the song meaning is ambiguous.
- Use Yes if the song uses irony, metaphor, rivalry, slang, or unclear narrative perspective in a way that affects classification.
- Use No only when the classification is reasonably clear.
